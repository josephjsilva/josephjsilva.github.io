Recognizing Phishing Attempts

Suspect an Email?

Forward any suspicious emails by using the "Forward as Attachment" function in Outlook to send to phishing@hceb.org immediately. Do not click links or download attachments.

Phishing emails are deceptive messages designed to steal your sensitive information, like passwords or financial details. Attackers disguise themselves as trustworthy sources. Be vigilant and look for these common red flags:

  • Urgent or Threatening Language: Phrases that create pressure, such as "account suspension" or "unauthorized login attempt," are common tactics.
  • Unexpected Financial Lures: Be wary of surprise messages about overdue invoices, tax refunds, or prizes. Scammers use these to provoke a quick, emotional response.
  • Suspicious Links & Senders: Hover over links to preview the actual URL. Check that the sender's email address matches the organization they claim to be from.
  • Generic Greetings & Errors: Look for vague greetings like "Dear User" and obvious spelling or grammar mistakes.

When in doubt, it's always better to be safe. Report the email and verify any requests through official channels.
